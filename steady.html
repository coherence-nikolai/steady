Steady

Before the reaction

Use this when things feel intense. Choose what's present.

Overwhelm
nervous system overload
Your system is overloaded.
Nothing is wrong with you.
This is a nervous system response — not a character flaw.
Feel your breath at the upper lip. Stay here for a moment.
When you're ready — one small step. Just one.
Procrastination
avoidance of discomfort
You're not lazy.
You're avoiding discomfort. That's what nervous systems do.
The task isn't the threat — the feeling before starting is.
Feel your hands. Notice the weight of them. Stay here.
Open the task. One sentence. One line. That's all.
Shame
a learned response
You are not the problem.
You are feeling shame. Those are different things.
Shame is learned. It lives in the body, not in the truth.
Feel your feet on the ground. Let the floor hold you.
One gentle step — toward something, not away.
Anger
a protection response
You are activated.
Your system is trying to protect you. That's its job.
The threat feels real. Action can wait.
Feel your feet pressing into the ground. Weight down. Stay here.
Do not act yet. Let the wave pass first.
Disconnection
a protective state
You're not broken.
Your system has gone offline. This is protection, not failure.
Numbness is a door — not a wall.
Rub your hands together slowly. Feel the warmth. Stay here.
One light action. Something small and physical.
